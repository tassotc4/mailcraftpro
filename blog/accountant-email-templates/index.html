5 Accountant Email Templates for Tax Season and Year-Round Client Communication

By HelpMeWrite · June 17, 2026 · 8 min read

Accountants communicate with clients across high-stakes moments — tax deadlines, financial reviews, audit notices, fee discussions. These templates help you communicate clearly and professionally at every stage of the client relationship, from onboarding to year-round updates.

What's covered in this article:

  • Tax deadline reminder
  • Document request email
  • New client engagement letter email
  • Client financial update
  • Referral request from a satisfied client

Tax deadline reminder

Template 1
Subject: Important: tax deadline approaching — action required
Dear [Client Name], I'm writing to remind you that [tax type — e.g. 'your personal income tax return'] is due on [date], which is [X weeks] away. To ensure we file on time, I will need the following from you by [date — 1-2 weeks before deadline]: ☐ [Document 1 — e.g. W-2s / P60 / payment summaries] ☐ [Document 2 — e.g. bank interest statements] ☐ [Document 3 — e.g. receipts for deductible expenses] ☐ [Document 4 — e.g. investment income statements] You can send these securely via [email / client portal / post]. If you're unable to gather everything in time, please let me know as soon as possible and we can discuss whether an extension is appropriate. Please don't leave this to the last minute — penalties for late filing can be significant. [Your Name], [CPA/credentials] [Firm Name] | [Phone]

Document request email

Template 2
Subject: Documents needed — [Client Name] — [Tax year/matter]
Dear [Client Name], To complete your [return / financial statements / audit], I require the following documents at your earliest convenience: 1. [Document 1] — [why needed / where to find it] 2. [Document 2] — [why needed / where to find it] 3. [Document 3] — [why needed / where to find it] Ideal receipt date: [date] Deadline: [hard deadline] You can send these via [secure email / our client portal at: link / post to our office]. If you have any difficulty locating any of these documents, please reach out and I can advise on alternatives. Thank you for your prompt attention to this. [Your Name] [Firm Name] | [Phone]

New client engagement letter email

Template 3
Subject: Engagement letter — [Service] — [Client Name]
Dear [Client Name], Thank you for choosing [Firm Name] to assist with your [accounting/tax] needs. We are pleased to welcome you as a client. Please find attached our engagement letter, which sets out: • The scope of services we will provide • Our fee structure and payment terms • Our respective responsibilities • How we handle your confidential information To formalise our engagement, please: 1. Read the engagement letter carefully 2. Sign and return the attached copy by [date] 3. Provide the documents listed in Schedule A Once received, we will open your file and begin work immediately. Please don't hesitate to contact me with any questions. We look forward to working with you. [Your Name], [CPA/credentials] [Firm Name] | [Phone] | [Email]

Client financial update

Template 4
Subject: Financial update — [Client Name] — [Quarter/Period]
Dear [Client Name], Please find below your financial summary for [period]. Key figures: • Revenue: $[amount] ([+/-X%] vs prior period) • Expenses: $[amount] • Net profit: $[amount] ([margin]%) • Cash position: $[amount] Highlights: • [Positive note 1 — e.g. 'Revenue is tracking ahead of last year'] • [Note 2 — e.g. 'Accounts receivable has improved by X days'] Items to watch: • [Item 1 — e.g. 'Tax instalments due [date] — set aside $X'] • [Item 2 — e.g. 'Consider reviewing your super contributions before year-end'] Next steps: [What you'll be doing / what you need from the client] Please don't hesitate to reach out if you'd like to discuss any of the above. [Your Name] [Firm Name]

Referral request

Template 5
Subject: A small favour — referrals
Dear [Client Name], I hope this finds you well. I wanted to reach out with a brief request. Our firm is growing, and the best clients we gain come through introductions from people like you — clients who understand the value of working with a trusted accountant. If you know a business owner, professional, or individual who might benefit from our services — particularly around [tax planning / business accounting / SMSF / etc.] — I'd be grateful for an introduction. I'm happy to offer a [complimentary initial consultation / fee credit / gift card] as a thank you for any referral that leads to a new engagement. And of course, if there's ever anything I can do to support you or someone in your network, please don't hesitate to ask. With thanks, [Your Name] [Firm Name]

Frequently asked questions

How do I write a tax deadline reminder that gets clients to act?

Be specific and create urgency without alarm. State the exact deadline, list precisely what you need from the client and by when, explain the consequences of missing the deadline briefly, and make it easy to respond. HelpMeWrite's tax deadline reminder template includes a clear checklist format that clients can act on immediately.

When should accountants send client financial updates?

Quarterly is best practice for business clients — it keeps them informed about their financial position and surfaces planning opportunities (like tax instalments or super contributions) before they become urgent. Annual updates alone are not enough for clients making ongoing business decisions.

How do I ask accounting clients for referrals professionally?

Ask after a successful outcome — a good tax result, a smooth audit, or a helpful financial review. Be specific about who you're looking to meet, offer something in return, and make the ask feel natural rather than transactional.
